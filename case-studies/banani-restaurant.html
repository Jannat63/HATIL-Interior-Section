The Challenge

Atmosphere First. Then Seating.

The restaurant owner's brief was clear: "When guests walk in, I want them to feel like they're somewhere special before they even look at the menu." The 2,400 sq.ft space in Banani needed to seat 80 covers comfortably while projecting a warm, upscale fine dining atmosphere.

The challenge was the shape of the space — a long, narrow floor with an awkward column in the middle. The interior design had to work with the column, not fight it, while creating a flow that maximised covers without feeling crowded.

The kitchen pass-through and bar area also had to be seamlessly integrated into the dining floor without disrupting the aesthetic.

Key Challenges

Our Approach

Warm Timber, Intimate Lighting, Considered Flow

HATIL's commercial interior team treated the awkward column as an opportunity — positioning it as a natural divider between the main dining floor and an intimate private dining alcove for 8–10 guests. This created two distinct moods within the same space.

The material palette centred on warm walnut-toned dining tables and cushioned chairs in deep burgundy fabric — all custom-manufactured at HATIL's plant to the exact dimensions of the floor layout. The bar counter used a solid wood HATIL unit with a custom stone top specified by the client.

Ceiling-height open shelving units on the feature wall created visual depth and display space — a signature detail that photographs well and anchors the room.

The Results

Fully Booked Within Two Weeks of Opening.
